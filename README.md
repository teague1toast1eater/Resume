# Interview

## What is an Interview

Questions you be able to answer in clear, concise manner:
0. can you do the job?
1. do you want to do the job?
2. will they enjoy working with you?

- showcase your skills, not just the basic log of what you did

### Info about job/company:
    - what does a typical day look like?
    - what are the biggest challenges facing the team right now?
    - how do you measure success in this role?
    - can you tell me more about the team I would be working with?
### Company culture:
    - how would you describe the company culture here?
    - what do you enjoy most about working here?
    - how does the company support professional development and growth?
### Opportunities for advancement:
    - what other compensation or benefits does the company offer?
    - what are the opportunities for growth and advancement within the company?
    - how do you support employees in their career development?
    - what is the typical raise/promotion schedule like?
### Work-life balance:
    - how does the company promote work-life balance for its employees?
    - are there any flexible work arrangements available?
### Use of AI:
    - how is AI being utilized within the company?
    - are there any initiatives or projects focused on AI development?
### Training and resources:
    - does the company provide any training or resources for employees to learn about AI?
    - are there opportunities to attend conferences or workshops related to AI?
### Diversity and inclusion:
    - how does the company approach diversity and inclusion in the workplace?
    - are there any employee resource groups or initiatives focused on promoting diversity?
### Next steps:
    - what are the next steps in the interview process?
    - is there anything else you need from me to help with your decision?
### Timeline for a decision?
### Ask follow up questions based on what they said earlier in the interview: this shows that you are listening and engaged.

At the end of the interview, don't ask for feedback on how you did. 
It can come across as unprofessional, and brings their mind back to the negative. 
Instead, focus on expressing your enthusiasm for the role and the company. 
End on on a good note.

## Q&A

What should I expect and say that my pay range should be? And how should I negotiate it?
- Research over negotiation for entry level roles: make sure that you are within the market range, don't low-ball yourself, but keep it reasonable
- Research the market rate for the position in your location
- Consider your experience, skills, and qualifications
- When asked about your salary expectations, provide a range based on your research
